10 Frequently Asked Questions about Kalaupapa National Park

>What are the means of transport to the park?
By air, mule, foot, and boat
What are the operational hours and season of the park?
Because it is still a living community, there are no opening or closing hours. Visitation is restricted in accordance with Hawaii State Law.
What causes Hansens disease leprosy?
A chronic, infectious disease caused by a bacillus, Mycobacterium leprae. This disease usually affects the nerves, skin and eyes.
When was the park established?
President Jimmy Carter signed Public Law 96-565 establishing the park on December 22, 1980.
Where is Kalaupapa National Historical Park?
The park is on the north shore of the island of Molokai, Hawaii, in Kalawao County
What is weather like during the different seasons?
Hawaii enjoys warm temperatures year-round, although site conditions at the park can vary depending on weather and time of year. The rainy season is in the winter with the more hot and humid days in the summer. Trade winds are fairly constant and typically blow from the northeast.
What is the physical address?
P.O. Box 2222 Kalaupapa , HI 96742
Do you need a permit to visit Kalaupapa?
Hawaii State law requires all individuals to secure a permit prior to entering Kalaupapa National Historical Park.
Is there a visitors center in the park?
No there is not a visitors center within the park, but thee is a bookstore with limited hours of operation
